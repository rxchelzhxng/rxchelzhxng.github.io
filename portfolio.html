MAYA

UW Nanorobotics Group - Fall 2020 to Present

Imagine if we could automate surgery or target drug delivery. This inspiration is driving the crux of performing complex operations at the nano-scale. We are currently prototyping and simulating the behaviour of the magnetic flux pinning yielded by our YBCO superconductor. I have worked on automating the microrobot's system in Java and translating the electric/magnetic response in COMSOL Multiphysics software.

Scanning Tunneling Microscope

Course Project - Mar 2020

What makes a scanning tunneling microscope (STM) special compared to a light microscope? An STM is used to achieve 3D atomic-scale images of materials using the quantum tunneling effect. I designed a vibration isolation system for the STM using SolidWorks in order to combat the sensitivity of ground-based vibration. Once the design was developed, the build for a scanner head assembly and current-amplifier circuit was en route. I was able to apply electrochemical etching lab techniques and produced a sharp tip that minimized vibration. As a result, I was able to achieve consistent tunneling current signals.

Mariokart Inspired Soapbox Car

Course Project - Dec 2019

As a final project to flesh out our 3D CAD skills after a semester of designing automobile parts, we modelled a full end-to-end design of a Mariokart inspired soapbox car using SolidWorks. I took lead in sketching and assembling the structural frame, wheels, and seats. I learned how to construct a steering mechanism and use Solidworks animation tools to exhibit the motion study.

Haptics Gait Training

UW Biomechatronics - Fall 2019

I've always had a passion for innovating technology in the healthcare sector. This lead me to join UW Biomechatronics Haptics team with a special focus in electrical hardware. Our mission was to use Haptics technology to train users with Parkinson's disease by providing feedback if their gait was not aligned. We prototyped a haptics wearable IMU sensor to track motion cycles and generated reports. I learned how to mount an MPU6050 and worked on troubleshooting Arduino sensor issues.

3D Printed Cell Model

ProtoMD - Winter 2020

Often times learning cell biology requires a visual, live model. With this driving need, we designed a 3D printed cell model to be used by high school biology laboratories in the KW region to articulate the steps of protein production. I outlined an interactive, moving part design to provide hands-on learning. Combing molecular biology and mechanical concepts, I also modelled the cell organelles, specifically the nucleus and nucleus pores in SolidWorks.
